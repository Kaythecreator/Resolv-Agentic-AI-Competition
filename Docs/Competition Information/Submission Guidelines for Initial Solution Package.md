### **2026 UMD Agentic AI Challenge**

### **Submission Guidelines for Initial Solution Package (Due April 15\)**

1. Form a team of 4 or 5 UMD students and register your team (use your **UMD** email credentials to register).   
2. Choose one of the challenge problems provided in the other tabs of this document or propose your own. Any self-defined problem **must** be grounded in a real-world business context and should demonstrate a level of depth and complexity comparable to those provided.   
3. Use a real or synthetic dataset, including data generated with an LLM if needed, to design and test your solution.  
4. Create a demo **video of up to 5 minutes** demonstrating your fully/party working prototype (a more complete solution will have a higher chance of being selected for the next stage). Upload the video on some cloud service (Google Drive, etc.) and submit the link. Please don’t forget to **provide access** to the link (a simple way to make sure is to open the link in incognito mode in your browser).  
5. Apart from the video link, submit a reproducibility and usage PDF (maximum  5 pages). A reader of this document should be able to both use your proposed system and reproduce your prototype. Name the file after your team’s name.   
6. If you have any other supporting files, you can submit them as well, via a link to your Google Drive.

### **Guidelines for Final Presentation and Solution (on April 24\)**

1. By April 18, selected teams will be invited to deliver a final working prototype  
2. Selected teams should incorporate feedback from the judges to improve their initial submission package.  
3. Selected teams must commit to a live presentation of their work on April 24\.  
4. More details will be distributed to selected teams.

### **Notes/FAQ**

1. Because novelty, methodology, and clarity are the primary criteria for evaluation, the competition is well-suited to all UMD students, regardless of major or academic level.  
2. Please submit only one demo video, not exceeding 5 minutes in total length. All other links must be submitted in the supporting document.   
3. There should be only one submission from the team (by the team captain).  
4. You can use free API credits provided by many LLM firms. We don’t partner with any LLM firms to provide such API credits.
